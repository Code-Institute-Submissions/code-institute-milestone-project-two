ABOUT BIELEFELD

Welcome to the heart of the East Westphalia-Lippe region, home to a number of big names including Dr. Oetker and Seidensticker, Alcina and JAB Anstoetz, Dürkopp, Gildemeister and Schüco. Some 325,000 people live here, making Bielefeld one of the twenty biggest cities in the Federal Republic. The city celebrates its 800th birthday in 2014. But apart from the anniversary year, there’s lots to explore here. Fascinating monuments to the city’s industrial past, like the Ravensberg Spinning Mill and the Linen Works Museum, are well worth a visit, as are Dr. Oetker World and the art centre, Kunsthalle Bielefeld. Go exploring – it’s worth it!


WHAT'S ON

In September Bielefeld's Old Town transforms into a cosy open air Wine Village: In atmospheric surroundings right in Bielefeld’s ”front room”, the Alter Markt, and at Niedernstraße and Altstädter Kirchplatz, tastings of a multitude of wines from Germany’s wine growing regions are held - accompanied by one or more culinary delights.

Medieval hustle and bustle with the backdrop of historic Sparrenburg Castle, Bielefeld’s favourite visitor destination. Every July, a wild troupe of magicians, storytellers, traders and vagrants bring to life the times of preachers selling indulgences, of minstrels and the Black Death, and of wanton high spirits. Hawkers, peddlers and craftsmen dominate the medieval market with their wares..

The "Leineweber-Markt" is Bielefeld’s oldest street fair with the richest traditions. For five days at the end of May, the city centre of Bielefeld transforms itself into an open air stage. Visitors from the region and far away enjoy an entertaining mix of street theatre, cabaret, folklore, jazz, rock and other shows – and all for free.
